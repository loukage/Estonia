About the Transformation

Estonia’s journey from Soviet censorship to a beacon of media freedom exemplifies resilience and innovation. After gaining independence in 1991, Estonia embraced democratic institutions, fostering an environment of press freedom and digital innovation.

Media Under Soviet Rule

During the Soviet era, Estonia’s media was strictly controlled by communist authorities. Outlets such as Rahva Hääl (People’s Voice) and Noorte Hääl (Youth Voice) acted as propaganda tools for the Communist Party.

Current Media Landscape

Today, Estonia is a leader in digital governance and media freedom. Major outlets include Postimees, Eesti Päevaleht, ERR, and Delfi. Estonia ranks 6th in the World Press Freedom Index.
